Faker: The Unmatched Legend

Lee "Faker" Sang-hyeok is widely regarded as the greatest *League of Legends* player of all time. Born on May 7, 1996, in Seoul, South Korea, Faker's career began in 2013 with T1 (then known as SK Telecom T1), where he continues to play to this day. His achievements and contributions to esports have made him a cultural icon and a symbol of excellence in competitive gaming.

Career Highlights

  • World Championships: Faker has won five World Championships (2013, 2015, 2016, 2023, and 2024), solidifying his legacy.
  • Mid-Season Invitational: Won the MSI in 2016 and 2017, making him one of the most decorated international players.
  • LCK Titles: Holds ten LCK titles, the highest number by any player in the history of the Korean league.

Records and Milestones

  • Reached 100 victories in World Championships in October 2024, the first player ever to do so.
  • Became the first player to achieve 500 kills at Worlds, a monumental milestone in competitive LoL.
  • Awarded the MVP title in the 2024 Worlds Final, marking his enduring impact on the game.

Legacy and Influence

Faker is known not only for his victories but also for his innovative playstyle and consistency. He is a player who has adapted to every meta change and continues to perform at the highest level over a decade into his career. Faker's influence extends beyond the game, making him a cultural figure in South Korea and a role model for aspiring esports athletes around the world.

Additional Facts

  • In 2020, Faker renewed his contract with T1 for three years and became a co-owner of the organization, underscoring his long-term commitment to the team.
  • He was recognized in the Forbes 30 Under 30 list in 2019, in the Entertainment and Sports category, highlighting his influence beyond esports.
